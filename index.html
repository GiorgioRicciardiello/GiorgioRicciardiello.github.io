About

I'm a Machine Learning Scientist and Biomedical Researcher with 5+ years building multimodal AI and signal-processing systems for neural, wearable, and clinical data. My pipelines span cohorts exceeding 2 million patients — from actigraphy-derived sleep biomarkers that predict Parkinson's disease a decade before diagnosis, to population-scale causal inference for stroke and dementia prevention.

Currently a Data Analyst II at Mount Sinai. Previously a Research Assistant in the Mignot Laboratory at Stanford. I hold an MS in Epidemiology & Clinical Research from Stanford, an MS in Biomedical Engineering from the Technical University of Denmark, and a BS in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Genoa.
